Week 5 Hackathon

Progress

This week, we participated in the ByteHackz Hackathon where we spent a grueling 2.5 days coding and collaborating to produce a product prototype for our sponsor, AIDC. For our prototype, we were able to produce a working RFID reader which included facial recognition that is connected to the Pi. Using an internal database in the Pi, we were able to add staff data that contained our unique RFID key, and facial recognition which the device was trained on earlier. With this data, once we tapped our RFID card, and the facial recognition device was able to recognise our faces, the speaker module was able to read out the staff data to signify that a record was added to the system. Finally, we presented our product to the stakeholder. Through the feedback, we have taken into consideration some improvements that are to be made in the future and we will work towards implementing a improved version of our product for our final submission. Through this experience I have found that our weakness is not being unable to deliver the product but having problems trying to present our work/product we are slightly unorgranised, unclear and there was also lacking information, this can be a problem in the future where we might have a revolutionary product but being unable to sell our product to clients can pose a threat. Thus, for our final presentation I have made it our goal to work on our presentation skills in order to present our product well to the client and convince them why they should believe in us.

Contributions

During the span of the 2.5 days, I managed to finalise my SQL database which involved many tweaks to ensure that the data could be written to and retrieved reliably. Additionally, I generated around 50 instances of test data for the SQL database to test and run and ensure the foreign keys and primary keys were properly assigned. Aside from the database, my groupmate and I also worked on a python console app which was intended to be the future interface for adding new staff and shifts into the database. Using this console app, we are able to add new staff, remove staff, add new shifts and remove shifts from the database. I had tried my best to ensure that our database is close to perfect and the different tables are relevant yet functional and the user will not run into any error while using the product as the database is the backbone of our product/application. I had also spent some time helping out with creating the slides for the presentation and figuring out the sequence of our presentation. Finally, I presented the SQL database that I had created and also present some of the Python Console App that my groupmate and I had worked on.
